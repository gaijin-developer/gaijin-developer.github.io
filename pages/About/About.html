About Frank

I am a certified Electrical and Electronics Engineer with experience working as a power plant operator and an electrical compliance officer.

I am a semi-self-taught web developer with practical experience in working with some major front end technologies including HTML, CSS, React and JavaScript and some backend technologies such as NodeJS, ExpressJS and PHP as well as the MySQL database. I am dedicated to writing clean, concise and reliable code to perform tasks and implement features that satisfy the needs of customers.

I had the idea to get into web development in early June 2021 when I had a conversation with a friend about exploring new ideas and skills and my friend suggested that we study software engineering. I did a little bit of research into what it meant to be a web developer and what it takes to be a good one. I later found a free resource that I could use to get started (freeCodeCamp). In the University, we had a course which was part of the electrical engineering curriculum - introduction to algorithms. What was taught in class wasn’t enough to properly introduce anyone to software engineering and what it entails.

In late June 2021, I started learning web development from freeCodeCamp through their responsive web design course. When I created my first app using just HTML (Cat Photo App) my eyes were opened to the endless possibilities and power this skill could offer me, I was so happy about being able to put the picture of the cat on the screen and to finally realize how all those sites I visit daily put pictures on their site.

I was very motivated to see the course through. I was working as a power plant operator at the time and finding time to study was a headache and so was keeping the motivation but I kept taking it a day at a time. I was encouraged by my wife (then girlfriend at the time) to keep going and do my best. Studying web development while working fulltime wasn’t easy but I kept on giving my best till my wife (then girlfriend) who is Japanese and I decided to meet in Ghana. I took a break to prepare for her arrival and to ensure that she enjoys her stay. After she left, it was difficult to find time to study and practice because I was still working full time while preparing for our wedding.

In March 2022, I got laid off from work which was difficult to take because I had just gotten married but it was also a great opportunity to get back to coding and studying. I resumed my studies full time and I decided to blend freeCodeCamp with other resources like Udemy and YouTube. I was able to improve a lot during this time period till I got an electrical engineering job in June 2022 but I was still determined to be a web developer since I found more joy in writing code and building amazing systems and I was also able to impress my wife very often with what I build. I have been using a combination of freeCodeCamp, Coursera and Udemy and YouTube to learn new technologies and improve my knowledge up till this day.

I am a very resilient and ambitious developer and I am committed to providing services and writing code that is up to standard and fitting to meet the demands of customers. I am dedicated to continuous learning and improvement in the field of software engineering. I have the goal to be a very skilled full stack developer by the end of 2023 with a substantial coverage of some key frontend and backend technologies as well as some tools such as docker. I seek to work for an institution/company that is willing to give me the room to reach my goals while making full use of my skills and knowledge. I also love to be challenged to go the extra mile to find the solution and I am never shy or embarrassed to say “I don’t know” and ask for help. I seek to work harder and get better every day.
